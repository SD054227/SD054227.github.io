What is Swimming

Swimming is an individual or team racing sport that requires the use of one's entire body with with the combined arm and leg motions and the natural flotation of the body to move through water. There are many events in swimming that range from 4 strokes that range from 25m to 1500m.

The History of Swimming

> In 1837, The first swimming organization was created in London. Then, Internationally, competitive swimming came into prominence with its inclusion in the modern Olympic Games from their inception in 1896. Since then, Swimming, as a sport, has continued to grow.

The Four Main Strokes of Swimming

Butterfly Stroke

  • To perform the butterfly stroke, repeatly, bring your arms simultaneously over your head and push them into the water as you dolphin kick to propel yourself through the water.

Backstroke

  • To perform the backstroke, while floating on your back, alternate your arms in a windmill-like motion as you flutter kick to propel yourself backwards.

Breasttroke

  • While on your stomach, move your arms simultaneously beneath the surface of the water in a half circular movement in front of your body while performing a frog like kick.

Freestyle

  • To execute the front crawl, you lie on your stomach while propelling yourself forward with alternating arm movements in a sort of windmill motion and simultaneously flutter kicking.
